About the Project

Drone detection, on-site marking, live coordination

Safestep Initiative combines drone-based sensing, real-time mapping, and autonomous coordination to make demining operations safer and more efficient. The system operates independently in hazardous zones where human access is risky.

Using metal and Hall-effect sensors, the UAV detects buried landmines and drops a dome marker that emits light and sound to warn nearby teams. All detection data is streamed instantly to a Flask dashboard, allowing operators to track missions and plan safe routes in real time.

Supported by UNODA, the project aims to deliver a scalable field-ready model that empowers humanitarian organizations and local demining teams.

Workflow

Autonomous Detection and Coordination

Safestep Initiative follows a fully autonomous three-step process — scanning the terrain, marking detected zones, and coordinating live updates through the mission dashboard.

1 Scan & Detect
2 Mark with Dome
3 Coordinate in Real-time
Technology

From sensors to live map

The Safestep Initiative system integrates metal and Hall sensors mounted on UAVs to precisely detect buried or surface-level landmines. Detected signals are continuously converted into structured event data and saved as CSV flight logs, enabling traceable mission records and easy analysis.

Through a Flask-based backend, the data is processed in real time and visualized on an interactive Folium map dashboard. This provides operators with instant geolocation feedback, safe-route suggestions, and real-time mission updates—helping teams make rapid, data-informed decisions in the field.

Impact

Built for real operations

Developed under the UNODA sponsorship, Safestep Initiative is engineered to enhance the speed, accuracy, and safety of humanitarian demining operations.

The platform not only improves detection efficiency but also provides a shared situational awareness among demining teams. Real-time coordination through the dashboard allows quicker decision-making, minimizing redundant work and ensuring safer clearance in high-risk zones.
